Instructions for Submitting an Application

  1. Signing and Disclosure Document. The applicant's required signer must sign and date the "Receipt" page at the end of the current Franchise Disclosure Document ("Disclosure Document") for the specific brand. This document must then be returned immediately by mail to the development representative.
  2. Application Completion. Every part of the application must be completed, and additional pages can be attached if necessary. All information must be legible, in English, and either typed or printed. The application can be filled out electronically, saved, and printed. However, a signed hard copy, along with the Application Fee (as detailed in Paragraph 5), must be submitted to the development representative.
  3. Supporting Documents. Applicants must attach all supporting documents and information indicated in each section of the application. If any part of the application is incomplete or supporting documentation is missing, a written explanation for the incompleteness or absence of documents must be provided. Incomplete applications will not be considered.
  4. Organizational Chart and Ownership. The applicant can be a natural person or an existing legal entity. A complete organizational chart must be provided, showing the structure up to the ultimate owning entity/entities and all ultimate individual beneficial owners of the applicant. Additionally, relevant Participant Information forms (Part 4) are required for the applicant and for any person or entity holding a 25% or greater ownership interest, or a controlling interest (e.g., general partner, managing member).
  5. Application Fee. A check or wire transfer for the "Application Fee" must accompany the application. Application fees vary by brand. Consult the Franchise Disclosure Document and fee schedule for each brand you intend to apply to. Fee information may also be available in the Dealality Brand Library. When you generate an Application Letter for a specific brand (see Part 2), you can reference that brand's fee requirements.

Required Signatures

The Application Letter must be signed and dated by the Applicant, or on behalf of the Applicant, by a person or persons with the capacity and authority to do so. The signatures required for valid execution of the Application Letter may vary depending on the laws under which the Applicant is established or resident. These laws must be complied with.
