Common Questions Answered

How are hotel properties valued? ▼
Hotel valuation is a complex process that considers multiple factors including RevPAR, occupancy rates, market conditions, location, property condition, and brand affiliation. The most common methods include:
  • Income Approach (DCF): Projecting future cash flows and discounting to present value
  • Market Comparables: Analyzing recent sales of similar properties in the same market
  • Cost Approach: Estimating replacement cost minus depreciation
What factors most impact hotel valuations? ▼
Key factors include location and market dynamics, RevPAR performance, occupancy rates, property condition and age, brand affiliation, management quality, and market timing. Location is often the most critical factor, followed by operational performance metrics.
What methodology does Dealality's calculator use? ▼
Our calculator combines income approach (DCF) analysis with market comparables, using hotel-specific metrics like RevPAR, occupancy, and ADR. It considers property type, location, market conditions, and operational performance to provide a realistic valuation range.
Can I use this valuation to sell my hotel? ▼
The valuation provides a solid starting point, but for an actual sale, we recommend engaging an experienced hotel M&A advisor who can perform a comprehensive analysis and guide you through the complex hotel transaction process.
